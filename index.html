Japan

What I ate

Yakitori from 'そね田' (soneta), a famous restaurant in Yamagata city.

Herring soba from ’水車生そば’（suishyaki soba) in Tendo city.

Miso ramen from ’居間人’ (imajin), my personal number one favorite.

Yamagata cherries, famous throughout all Japan. These are called satounishiki. They're sweet and sour.

Where I went

Yamadera shrine in Tendo City. After climbing several thousand steps, you can see some incredible things. This is about halfway up.

Ginzan Onsen is a hotspring in northern Yamagata prefecture. It's roads resemble previous eras with many shops to see and places to eat.

Yamagata is a snowy place, and during the winter you can see see many light shows, such as the one above.
